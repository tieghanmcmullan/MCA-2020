Task 2b): Discussion of the advantages of time-frequency analysis over waveform-based analysis

One of the main advantages of a time-frequency analysis over a wavefrom analysis is that it is easier to read and extract important info about the music from it. A spectogram allows us to visualise how the frequencies vary over the course of the song. Since specific notes correspond to a specific frequency, we can pick out which notes are being played.
However, waveforms contain masses of information, meaning it is difficult to pick out the information and difficult to read. Waveforms provide amplitude at a certain time, which correlates with loudness. The shape of the wave provides an indication of the changing frequency, but does not provide numerical data.
If you observe the waveforms for my songs it is clear that you can pick out a lot in formation regarding the song other than perhaps the loudness across the song which might then give an indication of when the verses and chorus take place.
However, if you know which particular frequency aligns with a particular pitch, then the spectogram means we are able to get an idea of how the tune goes.
