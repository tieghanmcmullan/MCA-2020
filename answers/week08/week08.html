Task 2.2: Advantages of a time-frequency analysis over a waveform-based analysis

One of the main advantages of a time-frequency analysis over a wavefrom analysis is that it is easier to read and extract important info about the music from it. A spectogram allows us to visualise how the frequencies vary over the course of the song. Since specific notes correspond to a specific frequency, we can pick out which notes are being played. However waveforms contain masses of information, meaning it is difficult to pick out the information and difficult to read it. Waveforms provide amplitude at a certain time, which correlates with loudness. The shape of the wave provides an indication of the changing frequency, but does not provide numerical data. For example, the waveform for (song) we can see that... we cant learn a lot about the song from the waveform apart from the changes in dynamics over time, which might at best give us an indication of perhaps when the chorus starts and ends (assuming that the chorus is usually the loudest part of the song, at least in the rock genre). By looking at the spectrogram, on the other hand, we can get an idea of the melody of the song if we know which frequency corresponds to which pitch.
